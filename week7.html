WOT IS PHYSICAL COMPUTING ?

WEEK 7 // RECAP

In Week 7, we visited the Telstra Creator Space to begin our exploration into the world of Physical Computing. It was our first hands-on session with real hardware, where we started understanding how code can interact with the physical world. Using the Arduino IDE software and a full Arduino kit, we learned how to set up a basic circuit, upload simple programs, and observe how components like LEDs and resistors work in response to digital instructions. This marked the beginning of a creative and technical journey—bridging the gap between programming and tangible experience. We discovered how sensors could detect inputs such as light or pressure, and how outputs like sound, vibration, or blinking lights could be used to communicate back. The Telstra Creator Space provided an exciting environment filled with tools, workbenches, and collaborative energy, making it an ideal place to begin building our first interactive prototypes. Through trial, error, and experimentation, we not only understood the basics of electronics but also the artistic potential of technology-driven interaction.
